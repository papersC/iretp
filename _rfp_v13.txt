1. Executive Summary

The Dubai Land Department (DLD) hereby issues this Request for Proposal (RFP) for the design,
development, deployment, maintenance, and post-go-live support of the Integrated Real Estate
Transparency Platform (IRETP). The primary strategic objective is to advance Dubai's standing in the
JLL Global Real Estate Transparency Index (GRETI) and to reinforce investor confidence among
international financial institutions, sovereign wealth funds, and global investors.

The platform directly supports the Dubai Economic Agenda D33 and the Smart Dubai Strategy by
providing open, accurate, real-time, and interactive real estate data to the public while equipping DLD
leadership with powerful internal risk management and developer oversight tools.

The IRETP comprises two integrated pillars:
    · External Public Portal -- A world-class, multilingual data portal for international investors,
         financial institutions, property developers, and the general public, offering interactive
         dashboards, a GIS map, an AI-powered data agent, self-service analytics, and open-data
         export capabilities.
    · Internal Management Platform -- A secure, role-based platform for DLD staff and
         leadership, encompassing a developer performance rating engine, Escrow account
         monitoring, an Early Warning Risk System (EWRS), and multi-level leadership alert
         workflows.

This RFP does not prescribe specific technology brands, frameworks, programming languages, or
hosting providers. Vendors are expected to propose the solution architecture, technology stack, and
hosting model that best meets the requirements described herein. Proposals are evaluated on 70%
technical merit and 30% budget and total cost of ownership.

Delivery is structured across four phases, all of which must be completed within 3 calendar months
from the date of contract signing. Go-live of any phase is conditional on the successful completion of
all mandatory testing and acceptance criteria for that phase -- no phase may go live before its tests
are fully passed. Following the go-live of the final phase, the vendor must provide a 12-month
warranty and technical support period as part of this contract.

2. Project Overview & Strategic Context

2.1 Background
The JLL Global Real Estate Transparency Index (GRETI), published biennially, is the primary
reference used by institutional investors, lenders, and multinational corporations to evaluate real
estate market quality globally. The 2024 edition assessed 256 individual indicators across 89
countries and 151 cities. Markets in the 'Highly Transparent' tier attract over 80% of global direct
commercial real estate investment. Dubai has demonstrated consistent improvement, and this
platform is designed to accelerate that progress.

5.3 AI Model Architecture -- Multi-Model Approach

DLD does not prescribe a specific AI model, vendor, or provider. The platform must support a multi-
model AI architecture that gives DLD the flexibility to use more than one AI model -- either
simultaneously for different tasks, or switchable by the platform administrator without requiring
redevelopment. The vendor must propose and justify their multi-model architecture in the proposal.

12.2 Pre-Publication Analytics Assessment

Before any data is made live on the External Portal, the vendor must complete a Pre-Publication
Analytics Assessment. This assessment is a mandatory gate -- no public data publication is permitted
until the assessment is completed and signed off by the DLD data liaison officer. The assessment
must validate:

    · End-to-End Data Reconciliation: platform figures match the authoritative DLD source system
         records for a minimum sample of 500 transactions drawn randomly across all property types,
         zones, and transaction types. Accuracy rate must be 99.5% or above.

    · Derived Metric Validation: every derived metric (price per sqft averages, rental yield %,
         zone-level aggregates, YoY change figures, Escrow adequacy ratios, developer scores) must
         be independently validated from raw data. Any discrepancy must be resolved before go-live.

    · Edge Case & Outlier Review: all statistical outliers must be identified, flagged to DLD, and
         handled (included, excluded, or annotated) by agreement before go-live.

    · Zone Boundary Verification: zone-level data aggregations use the correct official zone
         boundary definitions. No transactions double-counted or misassigned.

    · Trend Direction Validation: all trend indicators (up/stable/down arrows, YoY % changes)
         verified against raw data for correct direction and magnitude.

19. Data Governance, Privacy & Ownership

19.1 Data Ownership
    · All data processed, stored, or generated within the IRETP platform is the sole and exclusive
         property of Dubai Land Department.
    · The vendor has no rights to use any DLD data for any purpose other than delivering the
         contracted services.
    · Upon contract expiration, termination, or handover, the vendor must deliver all data in
         machine-readable, open formats within 30 calendar days and provide written certification of
         complete data destruction from all vendor-controlled systems within 60 days.

19.2 Personal Data Protection Compliance

    · The system must fully comply with UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the
         Protection of Personal Data (PDPL).

    · No personally identifiable information (PII) -- names, Emirates ID, nationality, or contact
         details -- may be displayed in any public-facing interface.

    · All transaction data displayed publicly must be either aggregated (zone/period level) or
         anonymised at the individual transaction level.

    · A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) must be signed between DLD and the vendor before
         any personal data is accessed.

    · User consent management for newsletters, alert emails, and cross-session AI memory must
         comply with PDPL.

20. Additional Recommended Features

The following features are recommended for inclusion in the platform to maximise GRETI score
improvement. Vendors may propose these as part of Phase 4 or as optional additions, with separate
costing where applicable:

    · International Market Benchmarking (Phase 4 -- included): comparison of Dubai's key
         metrics against 5 global peer cities (London, Singapore, New York, Paris, Hong Kong),
         sourced from publicly available official data and updated quarterly.

    · ESG / Sustainability Module (Phase 4 -- included): LEED, Estidama Pearl, and BREEAM
         certification status for applicable buildings and projects. The Sustainability sub-index showed
         the strongest improvement in GRETI 2024 and is a high-impact area for UAE ranking
         advancement.

    · Open Data API Portal (Phase 1 -- included): self-service API key registration for
         researchers, financial institutions, and PropTech developers. OpenAPI 3.0 documentation
         with interactive testing console.

    · Public Developer Scorecard (Phase 3 -- included): simplified public-facing version of the
         developer rating displaying completed projects, on-time delivery percentage, RERA
         compliance rating, and total units delivered.

    · Beneficial Ownership Transparency: display of publicly available corporate ownership
         structures for registered developers. Specifically cited in JLL's 2024 GRETI report as a key
         area for UAE improvement.

    · Mortgage & Debt Market Transparency: aggregate data on mortgage financing activity --
         total values registered, average LTV ratios by zone, and MoM trend. GRETI 2024 identified
         debt market transparency as a significant gap for UAE.
